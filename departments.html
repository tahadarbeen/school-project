Mechanical Engineering

Studies in this department began with the establishment of the college in 1961, offering a
general program until 1981. At that point, two specialized tracks were introduced: the
Mechanical Power Engineering program and the Mechanical Design and Production Engineering program.

Electrical Engineering

The Department of Electrical Engineering began its academic journey with the establishment of the college in 1961. In 1980,
two specializations were introduced: Electronics and Communications Engineering and Electrical Power and Machines Engineering.
In 1997/1998, a third specialization, Computer Engineering, was added, beginning in the third year with a focus on electronics and
communications. In 2017, two new credit-hour-based programs were introduced:
- Communications and Computer Engineering
- Electrical and Control Engineering

Civil Engineering

The Civil Engineering Department at Shoubra Faculty of Engineering, Benha University, was established in 1982.
Since its inception, it has become one of the leading academic departments contributing to the advancement of engineering education in
Egypt. The department was created to meet the growing demand for specialized civil engineers capable of addressing the needs of both
local and international markets effectively.

Architectural Engineering

Since its establishment in the 1960s, the Department of Architecture has continuously updated its curricula and developed its
regulations to keep pace with technological and informational advancements. This ensures that graduates are capable of competing
in both the local and international markets. The department focuses on preparing students who are creative in architectural and
urban design, possessing both manual and digital skills. Graduates are equipped to produce detailed and executive designs, as well
as handle tenders, contracts, and consider social, economic, and environmental factors. The department offers degrees in diploma,
master's, and doctoral programs in areas such as project management, building conservation and restoration, urban planning,
environmental design, housing, and architectural design studies.
